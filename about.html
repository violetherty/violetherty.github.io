Galleria Michela Rizzo Isola della Giudecca, Venezia, Italia

"The Others" by showcasing numerous 18th and 19th-century portraits along with related objects and textiles, skillfully merges elements of the past and the present, evoking in me a profound reflection on identities across time.

The Memorial Hall of the Victims in Nanjing Massacre by Japanese Invaders, Nanjing, China

This memorial hall, through detailed records and exhibitions, not only pays tribute to the victims but also prompts reflection on the horrors of war and calls for peace. Visiting it made me deeply aware of the importance of remembering history, cherishing peace, and never forgetting the brutality of war.

Galleria Giorgio Franchetti alla Ca' d'Oro

Marco Palmezzano's "Pietà con due angioletti" and Bernardino Zaganelli's "Madonna col Bambino e angeli musicanti" vividly capture maternal love and divinity in Christian art, profoundly moving and inspiring me both aesthetically and spiritually.

Basilica di San Marco, Venezia, Italia

Visiting St. Mark's Museum gave me a deep appreciation of its artifacts and historical significance, offering insights into Venetian Republic art and culture. It's a must-visit for history buffs and cultural explorers alike.
